Getting Started

How do I download and install DriveSwift? Popular
DriveSwift offers 4 specialized apps: DriveSwift Car, DriveSwift Motorcycle, DriveSwift ADI, and DriveSwift LGV/PCV. Each app is available for free on both iOS and Android devices. Visit the App Store or Google Play Store, search for the specific DriveSwift app you need, and tap "Get" or "Install". Each app is approximately 150MB and requires iOS 13.0+ or Android 7.0+.
Do I need to create an account to use DriveSwift?
You can use DriveSwift in guest mode with limited features, but creating a free account unlocks progress tracking, personalized study plans, and sync across devices. Premium features require a subscription.
Which DriveSwift app should I download?
Choose the app that matches your license type:
• DriveSwift Car - Car theory test (Category B)
• DriveSwift Motorcycle - Motorcycle theory test (Category A)
• DriveSwift ADI - Approved Driving Instructor Part 3 test
• DriveSwift LGV/PCV - Large Goods Vehicle and Passenger Carrying Vehicle tests (Categories C & D)

Hazard Perception

Are the hazard perception clips DVSA licensed content?
Yes, DriveSwift includes 36 DVSA licensed CGI (Computer Generated Imagery) hazard perception clips. These are the same type of clips used in the actual DVSA hazard perception test.
How does the hazard perception scoring work?
The scoring system mirrors the real DVSA test. You score points for clicking during the hazard window (0-5 points per hazard). Clicking too early, too late, or too frequently results in zero points. The maximum score per clip is 5 points.
What makes a developing hazard?
A developing hazard is a situation that requires you to take some action, such as changing speed or direction. It's something that causes you to respond as a driver - like a pedestrian stepping into the road or a car pulling out of a junction.

Technical Support

DriveSwift won't start or keeps crashing, what should I do?
First, try force-closing and restarting the app. If that doesn't work, restart your device and ensure you have the latest app version. Check you have at least 1GB of free storage space. If problems persist, contact support with your device model and iOS/Android version.
Can I use DriveSwift offline?
Yes! DriveSwift offers full offline functionality. Download content packs while connected to Wi-Fi, then practice theory questions and hazard perception anywhere without internet connection. Progress syncs when you reconnect.
Which devices are supported?
DriveSwift supports iPhone (iOS 13.0+), iPad (iPadOS 13.0+), and Android devices (Android 7.0+). The app is optimized for phones but works great on tablets too.

DVSA Content

How do I know the content is licensed by DVSA?
DriveSwift uses DVSA licensed content. You can verify this by contacting DVSA directly. All our theory questions and hazard clips come directly from DVSA's licensed question bank.
How often is the content updated?
Our content is updated regularly to reflect Highway Code changes and DVSA revisions. Major updates typically happen when DVSA releases new content, usually 2-3 times per year. App updates are pushed automatically.
Is the Highway Code the latest version?
Yes, DriveSwift includes the latest 2024 edition of the Highway Code with all recent updates including new rules for electric vehicles, autonomous vehicles, and updated guidance on mobile phone use.
