Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect your personal information

Last updated: September 9, 2025

DriveSwift ("we", "us", or "our") is committed to protecting your privacy and personal data. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, process, and protect your information when you use our mobile application and services.

Your Privacy Rights

We comply with UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018. You have the right to access, rectify, erase, and port your personal data. Contact us anytime to exercise these rights.

1. Information We Collect

Personal Information You Provide

  • Account Information: Name, email address, password
  • Profile Data: License type, learning preferences, study goals
  • Contact Information: When you contact support or provide feedback
  • Payment Information: Processed securely through App Store/Google Play

Automatically Collected Information

  • Usage Data: Questions attempted, scores, time spent studying
  • Device Information: Device type, operating system, app version
  • Performance Data: App crashes, loading times, feature usage
  • Analytics Data: Aggregated usage patterns and app improvements

DVSA Content Interaction

  • Theory Practice: Questions answered, categories studied, performance metrics
  • Hazard Perception: Video interactions, scoring data, reaction times
  • Progress Tracking: Study streaks, achievements, completion rates

2. How We Use Your Information

Service Provision

  • Provide access to DVSA licensed theory test content
  • Track your learning progress and identify weak areas
  • Sync your data across multiple devices
  • Deliver personalized study recommendations

Communication

  • Send important app updates and notifications
  • Respond to your support requests and inquiries
  • Share study tips and motivational content (with consent)

Improvement & Analytics

  • Analyze app usage to improve features and performance
  • Conduct research to enhance learning effectiveness
  • Fix bugs and technical issues
  • Develop new features based on user needs

Educational Use

We may analyze anonymized learning patterns to improve our educational content and help other users learn more effectively. No personal data is shared in these analyses.

3. Legal Basis for Processing

Under UK GDPR, we process your personal data based on:

  • Contract Performance: To provide the DriveSwift service you've subscribed to
  • Legitimate Interest: To improve our service, prevent fraud, and ensure security
  • Consent: For marketing communications and optional features (you can withdraw anytime)
  • Legal Obligation: To comply with applicable laws and regulations

4. How We Share Your Information

We Do Not Sell Your Data

We never sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

Limited Sharing for Service Provision

  • DVSA: Anonymous usage statistics to improve official content (no personal data)
  • Cloud Services: AWS/Google Cloud for secure data storage and sync
  • Analytics: Anonymous usage data to improve app performance
  • Customer Support: Zendesk for support ticket management

Legal Requirements

We may disclose information if required by law, court order, or to protect our rights and safety.

5. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your data:

  • Encryption: All data encrypted in transit and at rest
  • Access Controls: Strict access limitations for employee data access
  • Secure Infrastructure: SOC 2 compliant cloud hosting
  • Regular Audits: Periodic security assessments and updates
  • Incident Response: Procedures for handling any security incidents

Security Breach Notification

In the unlikely event of a data breach affecting your personal information, we will notify you within 72 hours as required by UK GDPR.

6. Your Privacy Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the following rights:

  • Right of Access: Request copies of your personal data
  • Right to Rectification: Correct inaccurate personal data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your personal data
  • Right to Restrict Processing: Limit how we use your data
  • Right to Data Portability: Receive your data in a portable format
  • Right to Object: Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent for consent-based processing

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at privacy@driveswift.co.uk

7. Data Retention

  • Active Accounts: Data retained while your account is active
  • Inactive Accounts: Data deleted after 3 years of inactivity
  • Support Data: Support communications retained for 2 years
  • Analytics Data: Anonymized data retained for service improvement
  • Legal Requirements: Some data retained longer if required by law

You can request immediate deletion of your account and data at any time.

8. Cookies and Tracking

DriveSwift uses minimal tracking technologies:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for app functionality and security
  • Performance Cookies: Anonymous analytics to improve the app
  • No Marketing Cookies: We don't use cookies for advertising

See our Cookie Policy for detailed information.

9. Children's Privacy

DriveSwift is designed for users aged 17 and older who are eligible to take the UK theory test. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 16 without parental consent.

If you believe we have collected information from a child under 16, please contact us immediately.

10. International Data Transfers

Your data is primarily stored within the UK and EEA. When we transfer data internationally, we ensure:

  • Transfers are to countries with adequate data protection laws
  • Appropriate safeguards are in place (Standard Contractual Clauses)
  • Your rights and protections travel with your data

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy occasionally. When we make significant changes:

  • We'll notify you via email or app notification
  • We'll update the "Last Modified" date at the top
  • We'll highlight key changes for 30 days

Continued use of DriveSwift after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
