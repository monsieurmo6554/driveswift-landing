Official DVSA Partnership

DriveSwift is proud to be an official partner with the Driver and Vehicle Standards Agency (DVSA), ensuring our content meets the highest standards for UK driving test preparation.

1. DVSA Partnership Overview

The Driver and Vehicle Standards Agency (DVSA) is the UK government agency responsible for improving road safety through testing drivers, supervising driving instructors, and conducting vehicle tests and approvals.

2. Licensed Content Details

2.1 Hazard Perception Materials

DriveSwift has been granted a license to use official DVSA hazard perception clips, including:

  • 36 Official CGI Clips: High-quality computer-generated imagery clips used in the official DVSA hazard perception test
  • Practice Scenarios: Additional practice clips developed in collaboration with DVSA
  • Scoring System: Official DVSA scoring methodology and timing systems
  • Instructions and Guidance: Official test instructions and preparation materials

2.2 Theory Test Questions

Our theory test practice includes:

  • Questions sourced from the official DVSA question bank
  • Case study questions and multiple-choice formats
  • Official explanations and Highway Code references
  • Regular updates to reflect current UK driving laws and regulations

2.3 Highway Code Materials

Licensed Highway Code content includes:

  • Complete text of the official UK Highway Code
  • Official road signs and markings
  • Traffic rules and regulations
  • Safety guidelines and best practices

3. License Terms and Restrictions

3.1 Permitted Use

The DVSA licensed content in DriveSwift may only be used for:

  • Personal study and driving test preparation
  • Educational purposes within the DriveSwift application
  • Practice testing and skill development
  • Progress tracking and performance analysis

3.2 Prohibited Activities

Users are strictly prohibited from:

  • Copying, downloading, or extracting DVSA content from the app
  • Redistributing or sharing licensed materials outside the app
  • Commercial use of DVSA content without separate licensing
  • Reverse engineering or attempting to access source materials
  • Creating derivative works based on DVSA content
  • Using content for purposes other than personal study

4. Content Accuracy and Updates

4.1 Content Verification

All DVSA licensed content in DriveSwift is:

  • Verified for accuracy by DVSA-approved processes
  • Regularly reviewed and updated to reflect current regulations
  • Aligned with the latest UK driving standards and requirements
  • Quality controlled through official DVSA channels

4.2 Update Schedule

Content updates are synchronized with DVSA releases:

  • Highway Code Updates: Within 30 days of official publication
  • Question Bank Changes: Quarterly synchronization with DVSA systems
  • Regulatory Changes: Immediate updates for critical safety information
  • Hazard Perception: Annual review and content refresh
